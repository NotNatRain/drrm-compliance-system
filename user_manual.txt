Drrm Compliance System - User Manual

1. Drrm Compliance System (General)

1.1 Login Page
Overview: The entry point of the system where users authenticate their identity to access school safety data.
- Access the system via the provided URL.
- Log in using your email and password.
- Roles: Admin (Full access), Contributor (School-specific), Viewer (Read-only).

1.2 Drrm Compliance Main Dashboard - Compliance Tab
Overview: A high-level overview of the entire DRRM system, showing compliance statuses across all modules.
- Central hub for all safety modules.
- Click on any module card (Fire Safety, Typhoon/Flood, Incident Checklist, CSS) to enter.
- Displays high-level compliance percentages for the selected school.

1.3 Drrm Compliance Main Dashboard - Schools Tab
Overview: A directory of schools within the division, allowing admins to manage school data and view specific school profiles.
- Admin: Manage all schools, view school IDs, and overall statuses.
- Contributor: View assigned school details.
- Search bar: Find schools by name or ID.

1.4 User Page
Overview: Centralized user management for controlling system access, adding new staff, and assigning roles/schools.
- Manage accounts and roles.
- Admins can add new users and assign them to specific schools.

1.5 Activity Log
Overview: A transparency tool that tracks all administrative actions to maintain accountability within the system.
- Audit trail for all system changes.
- Records who, what, and when.

1.6 Create System Announcement Modal
Overview: A tool for broadcasting critical updates or emergency notifications to all users in the system.
- Click the notification/announcement button to broadcast messages.
- Enter a title and content to inform all users about system updates or emergencies.


2. Fire Safety Compliance System

2.1 Dashboard Page
Overview: A management console for monitoring fire safety compliance across all schools in the division.
- Division Cards:
  - Passed Inspections: Schools that are 100% compliant.
  - Ongoing Improvement: Schools with active safety issues.
  - Unconfigured: Schools waiting for initial setup.
  - Total Schools: Overall count in the division.
- Buttons:
  - Generate Report: Download a division-wide summary.
  - Print/Success: Actions for individual schools to view or print details.

2.2 Building Page
Overview: Detailed management of school structures, including floor levels, room counts, and safety feature tracking.
- Add Building: Click "Add Building", enter code, floors, and rooms.
- Update Building: Click "View Details / Update" to change info or add safety features.
- Alarm Fire Extinguisher: Indicators on the building card show equipment status.
- Rooms: Displays the breakdown of rooms created for that building.
- Division Cards (Summary):
  - Total Buildings, Compliance Ratio, Active Alarms.
- History Button: View records of removed buildings, floors, or rooms.
- Inspection: "Inspect Now" records drills and checklist results.

2.3 Alarm Management
Overview: Tracks the installation, functionality, and maintenance schedule of fire alarm systems across the campus.
- Add Alarm: Specify code, type, and buildings covered.
- Update Alarm: Mark as functional or needs maintenance.
- Record Test: Set the last test date for maintenance tracking.

2.4 Extinguisher Page - Room Management
Overview: Ensures that every classroom and office has adequate safety coverage by mapping rooms to their respective buildings.
- Division Cards:
  - Total Rooms: Count of rooms in the building database.
  - Room Coverage Status: Percent of rooms with a nearby extinguisher.
- Add Room: Create a new room entry for a specific building/floor.
- Update Room: Inspect the room for secondary exits and smoke detectors.
- History Button: Access logs of previous room configurations.

2.5 Extinguisher Page - Extinguisher Management
Overview: Monitors the inventory, location, and maintenance status of fire extinguishers to ensure immediate availability.
- Add Extinguisher: Provide code, type, and its primary "Center Room".
- Assign Extinguisher: Select up to 3 rooms covered by the unit.
- Update/Delete: Change pressure levels or status (OK, Used, Missing). 
- Decommission: Use the removal option to archive the unit.
- History Button: View all inspection logs for extinguishers.

2.6 Evacuation Plan Page
Overview: Central repository for school-wide and building-specific evacuation maps and emergency protocols.
- Division Cards:
  - Shows how many buildings have uploaded plans and how many are missing.
- Evacuation Plan Tab:
  - Upload/View maps showing egress routes for each floor.
- Evacuation Site Tab:
  - Manage and locate the assembly area/safe zone for the school.

2.7 Customization Page
Overview: A management page for administrators to define system-wide parameters such as equipment types and operational statuses.
- How to Customize: Navigate to the Customization page via the sidebar or settings icon.
- Add & Edit: Use the "Add New" buttons to create new alarm types or extinguisher classifications. Click the "Edit" icon on existing items to update their labels or active status.
- Purpose: Ensures the system terminology matches the school's actual inventory and local safety standards.

2.8 Full Fire Safety Report
Overview: A comprehensive, printable summary of a school's entire fire safety infrastructure and compliance status.
- Printing Layout: Accessed via the "Full Report" button. It opens a clean, document-ready view.
- What it Displays:
  - School Information & Profile.
  - Summary of All Buildings (Floors, Rooms, Compliance).
  - List of All Installed Alarms and their last test dates.
  - List of All Fire Extinguishers and their pressure levels.
  - Overall Compliance Rating and pending safety issues.

2.9 Notification Page
Overview: A dedicated hub for tracking automated safety alerts, scheduled maintenance, and administrative messages.
- What it Displays:
  - Maintenance Alerts: Notifications for upcoming alarm tests or expired extinguishers.
  - System Announcements: Messages from the division administrator.
  - Activity Updates: Logs of recent room inspections or building updates.
- Usage: Use the "Mark as Read" feature to manage your task list and ensure no safety deadline is missed.


3. Typhoon Flood Monitoring System

3.1 Dashboard
Overview: A real-time monitoring screen for weather alerts and the active status of evacuation centers during disasters.
- Screening: Displays current weather alerts, active monitoring status, and active typhoon tracks.
- Buttons:
  - Add Center: Opens modal to register a new Evacuation Center.
  - Active Centers: Filter to see only currently occupied sites.
  - Status Indicators: Red (Extreme Warning), Yellow (Watch), Blue (Normal).

3.2 Report Posting
Overview: Facilitates the generation of official monitoring reports for submission to local government or education authorities.
- Generate and save monitoring reports as PDF.
- Click "Generate PDF" or the "Print" icon to create a formal document of the current status.

3.3 Dashboard - Evacuation Centers & Register Family
Overview: Manages the registration and allocation of evacuees to safe zones during emergency situations.
- Center Display: Shows current occupancy, capacity, and status (Operating/Standby).
- Register Family Modal: 
  - Fill in Head of Family, No. of Members, and specific needs (Pillows, Blankets, Food, etc.).
  - Select an existing school/center for assignment.

3.4 Evacuation Center Page
Overview: A detailed view of a specific evacuation site, including family demographics and supply needs.
- Displays: Breakdown of families, member counts, and specific needs inventory.
- Buttons:
  - Add Needs: Request/Update supplies for families.
  - Transfer Family: Move family to a different center if needed.
  - Evacuate Family: Mark family as having returned home safely.

3.5 Notification Page
Overview: A history of weather advisories and administrative alerts related to disaster monitoring.
- Displays system-wide weather alerts and manual announcements from administrators.


4. Incident Checklist System

4.1 Dashboard - Administrator
Overview: The main control panel for logging and tracking campus incidents and compliance events on a global calendar.
- Log Incident: Click "Log New Incident" to record date, type (e.g., Flood, Fire), and affected school.
- Calendar: Interactive view of all logged events. Click any date to view or add details.
- Legend Filters: Toggle view for specific types (Rainfall, Landslide, Accident, etc.).

4.2 Dashboard - Contributor
Overview: A specialized reporting interface for school-level staff to submit daily logs and incident reports.
- Differentiator: Restricted to viewing and reporting for their assigned school only.
- How To Use: Submit daily incident/compliance reports for approval.
- Buttons:
  - Pending Review: Shows reports waiting for Admin approval.
  - Registered: Confirmed reports appearing on the calendar.
  - Rejected: Reports sent back with a reason for correction.

4.3 Quick Checklist & Data Chart
Overview: Visual analytics and task management tools to help users track progress and export safety data for reporting.
- Printing: Click "Print Analytics" or the "Print" icon on charts to export pie/column data.
- Calendar Printing: Use "Print Calendar" to get a hard copy of the monthly log.
- Quick Checklist: Sidebar for tracking daily safety tasks; click items to mark as done.


5. Comprehensive School Safety (CSS) System

5.1 Dashboard
Overview: A snapshot of Comprehensive School Safety metrics, focusing on student totals and facility conditions.
- Kpi Cards: Real-time count of Assessments Completed, Total Students, and Facilities.
- School Info: Displays basic demographic and contact data for the active school.

5.2 Assessment Page
Overview: An interactive checklist based on national safety pillars to evaluate a school's overall disaster readiness.
- How To Assess: 
  - Click "Start New Assessment".
  - Fill in General Info (Assessor, Date).
  - Navigate through Pillars using the sticky menu.
  - Rate items (Yes/No), assign Points, and add Remarks.
- Buttons:
  - Pillar Links: Instant jump to specific checklist sections.

5.3 Edit Assessment Page
Overview: Allows assessors to refine previous evaluations as school conditions change or improvements are made.
- Click "Edit" on a completed assessment to update ratings or remarks.
- Totals are automatically recalculated upon saving.

5.4 Facility Page
Overview: Integrates physical facility data with safety findings to create a comprehensive risk register.
- How To Use: Register assembly areas and generic school structures.
- Data Displayed: 
  - Risk Register: Summary of safety indicators pulled from Fire Safety buildings.
  - Evacuation Plan: The school-wide protocol for emergencies.

5.5 Analytics & Reports Page
Overview: Compiles assessment data into actionable summaries and historical archives for long-term planning.
- Data Reported: Compliance rates, average safety scores, and student/facility totals.
- Buttons:
  - Print Assessment: Detailed questionnaire report for a specific date.
  - Print Safety Index: Scorecard overview for all school buildings.
  - Print Archives: Historical timeline of safety assessments.

5.6 Printing Layout
Overview: Standardized templates for generating high-quality physical copies of assessments and safety scorecards.
- Assessment: High-resolution table of all checklist criteria and responses.
- Safety Index: Visual summary of building conditions and priority findings.

5.7 Storage
Overview: An inventory management system for emergency supplies, medical kits, and rescue equipment.
- How To Use: Manage the inventory of emergency equipment (tools, medicine, supplies).
- Item Concerns: Track if items are Functional, Available, and where they were sourced from.
- Print Report: Official inventory list for physical audits.


6. Hazard Mapping

6.1 Dashboard
Overview: A visual mapping tool that provides multi-dimensional views of hazards and structural data across the school grounds.
- Map Navigation:
  - Entire School: View overall stats and building-level safety features.
  - Building Model: View a vertical stack of floors and finding counts.
  - Floor View: Topdown layout of rooms, hallways, and stairs.
- Primary Concerns:
  - Hazards/Vulnerabilities: Identifying high-risk areas per floor.
  - Room Allocation: Viewing specific inventory (chairs, tables, TV) inside each room.
